<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>My email address is spelled out in the curl license</li>



<li>The curl license appears in many products</li>



<li>Some people have problems with their products and need someone to email</li>



<li>A few of these discover my email in their product</li>



<li>Occasionally, the person in need of help emails me about their product.</li>



<li>I collect some of those and make them public</li>
</ol>



<p>I hope I don&#8217;t have to spell it out but I will do it anyway: in these cases I don&#8217;t know anything about their products and I cannot help them. Quite often I first need to search around only to figure out what the product is or does, that the person asks me about.</p>



<p>Over the years I have collected such emails that end up in my inbox. Out of those that I have received, I have cherry-picked my favorites: the best, the weirdest, the most offensive and the most confused ones and I <a href="https://daniel.haxx.se/email/">put them up online</a>. A few of then also triggered separate blog posts of their own in the past.</p>



<p>They help us remember that <strong>the world is complicated and hard to understand</strong>.</p>



<p>Today, my online collection reached the magical amount: 100 emails. The first one in the stash was received in 2009 and the latest arrived just the other day. I expect I&#8217;ll keep adding occasional new ones going forward as well.</p>



<p>Enjoy!</p>
